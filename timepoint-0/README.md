# Timepoint 0 — The First Noticed World

**Status:** Active canon foundation  
**Project:** Triquel / Paramecia  
**Purpose:** Record ecological origins before roads, towns, formal diplomacy, and long-distance migration.

## Core premise

Timepoint 0 begins with biome, proximity, and relation. Peoples arise within distinct ecological conditions, form local friendships at shared edges, and develop crosskin traditions through repeated contact.

Crosskin are relational before biological. They are shaped by proximity, choice, memory, mutual adaptation, and shared becoming.

## Folder map

- `canon/` — approved setting truths and canon locks
- `data/` — machine-readable indexes and taxonomies
- `maps/` — map inventory, visual references, and future asset manifests
- `systems/` — movement, settlement, ecological exchange, and relationship models
- `research/` — audits, contradictions, comparisons, and expansion questions

## Layer sequence

1. Origin biomes and starting peoples
2. Kin-to-biome relationships
3. First friendships and alliances
4. Deep kin relations
5. First cross-species kin
6. Movement taxonomy
7. First settlement logic

## Canon laws

- Relationship precedes institution.
- Crosskinship is not ownership or assimilation.
- Every origin bond must emerge from neighboring or overlapping biomes.
- Every bond creates a shared practice.
- Every relation changes the place as well as the people.
- No path becomes a cage.

> We are not the same. We are not the one. We are the many, together.

**Many roots. Many paths. Many kin. One worlding.**
