# The Lantern Inheritance

System: Dungeons & Dragons 5e compatible
Format: One-shot
Recommended level: 5
Party size: 3-6
Duration: 4-6 hours
Tone: civic mystery, relational fantasy, light danger, restorative ending

## Premise

During the Night of a Thousand Lanterns, the Grand Codex Founders portrait awakens. Its figures turn toward the room, but one place in the composition has gone blank. At the same moment, the Quiet Room begins losing its warmth, the Listening Seats stop carrying the sound of rain, and the Shelf of Unfinished Things sheds its bookmarks onto the floor.

The Codex writes a single line:

> A lantern was inherited as authority instead of responsibility.

The characters must discover which civic tradition has fossilized, restore its living purpose, and decide what the next generation should inherit.

## Core tension

A newly appointed steward, Caldrin Vale, has interpreted an old Founders' protocol as a mandate to standardize care across Hells Branch. His intentions are sincere. His policies have made welcome measurable, rest scheduled, and help mandatory.

Nothing is visibly broken.

People have simply stopped breathing freely.

## Accord sequence

1. Recognition: What is actually here?
2. Midnight: What must be honored?
3. Harmony: What wants to flourish?

## Act I: The portrait moves

The party attends the lantern ceremony in the Great Hall. Read or paraphrase:

> Gold light runs through the painted lanterns. One by one, the Founders turn their faces away from the empty ceremonial chair. The open Codex paints over its own final sentence and replaces it with a question: Who turned care into command?

Clues:

- the Quiet Room entrance sign now reads "Rest Period: Authorized Occupants Only"
- blankets appear beside people who did not request them
- Welcome Stewards carry checklists instead of introductions
- Lark Softvoice reports that visitors have started asking permission to be silent

## Act II: Three civic trials

The party may complete these in any order.

### Trial of Recognition: The Listening Seats

The rain has gone silent because the room is amplifying official guidance instead of the weather.

Challenge:

The characters must spend ten uninterrupted minutes listening to three residents without solving, correcting, or assigning tasks.

Use three short testimonies:

- a caretaker who is tired of being thanked instead of relieved
- a newcomer who was welcomed efficiently but never personally known
- an apprentice who abandoned a project because every pause became a progress review

Success:

The rain returns. Each character gains Inspiration.

### Trial of Midnight: The Shelf of Unfinished Things

Caldrin has marked the shelf for "completion processing." Its objects begin fading.

Challenge:

The party must recover three unfinished works and learn why each was paused.

Examples:

- a scarf paused during grief
- a letter paused until anger softened
- a map paused because the cartographer no longer recognized the destination

Success:

The plaque restores itself:

> You are allowed to come back later.

The party receives the Witness Stitch token.

### Trial of Harmony: The Willow Hearth

The fire burns too hot, magically forcing creatures nearby to gain the benefits of a short rest while also preventing them from leaving.

Challenge:

The party must reduce the fire without extinguishing it. Solutions may involve Nature, Arcana, Persuasion, tea ritual, music, or sharing responsibility among several people.

Success:

Sage Willow appears and says:

> Care that cannot be declined is only control wearing a blanket.

## Optional combat: The Fossilized Founder

If the party confronts Caldrin with accusation rather than inquiry, the portrait extrudes an armored figure made of varnish, parchment, and rigid golden roots.

Use the Helmed Horror stat block with these changes:

- replace Flying Sword immunity with immunity to charm and fear
- when damaged, it recites a rule that once protected someone
- a character can use an action and succeed on a DC 15 Persuasion, History, or Insight check to name the living need beneath that rule; three successes end the encounter peacefully

Killing the construct does not solve the crisis. It reforms unless the underlying protocol is renewed.

## Act III: Caldrin Vale

Caldrin is not corrupt. He is frightened.

Years earlier, someone was harmed because care depended on improvisation. He created standards so no one would be overlooked again.

His error was treating observation as authority.

The party must help distinguish:

- guaranteed access from mandatory participation
- visible support from surveillance
- standards of availability from standardized people
- responsibility from control

## Final choice

The Codex offers three revisions:

1. Abolish the protocol completely.
2. Preserve the protocol unchanged.
3. Rewrite it with refusal, correction, pause, and local adaptation built into its operation.

The third option best reflects Triquel, but the party should articulate the exact new language.

Suggested final text:

> Care must be available, visible, and accountable. It must also remain invitational. Observation reveals responsibility, not authority.

## Resolution

The blank figure returns to the portrait, but its face remains unfinished. The image leaves room for whoever next practices stewardship without claiming ownership of it.

The Quiet Room exhales. Rain returns to the windows. Blankets stop chasing people around the hall.

## Rewards

### Witness Stitch

Once per long rest, a character may add 1d6 to an ability check made to repair, reconcile, remember, or continue something previously broken or abandoned.

### Quiet Room Welcome

The party always has access to rest, tea, witness, and safe lodging in Hells Branch.

### Title: Lantern Successor

The title grants no rank. It marks someone trusted to ask what a system protects before deciding how it should operate.

## Key NPCs

### Sage Willow

Deerkin Rest Keeper. Calm, observant, and firm when care becomes coercive.

### Lark Softvoice

Assistant steward. First to notice that the Quiet Room's silence has become permission-seeking rather than restful.

### Caldrin Vale

Recognition Accord administrator. Sincere, intelligent, frightened of preventable harm, and overly attached to standardization.

### Prism Aurorasong

Found at the Listening Seats. Helps players recognize feelings that have not yet become arguments.

## GM principles

- no villain is required
- every rule began as an attempt to protect something
- listening should produce actionable information
- refusal is a valid response
- rest is part of play, not dead time
- the best ending changes both the institution and the people maintaining it
