# Ravenkin Culture Canon

**Status:** Canonical  
**Canon level:** Core  
**Last reviewed:** 2026-07-21

## Core Identity

Ravenkin are the **Circle of Significance**. Their work is to witness, preserve, interpret, and carry what must not be lost.

Their defining ethic is:

> Witnessing does not create ownership.

They do not claim authority merely because they observed an event. They preserve context, testimony, contradiction, ordinary life, grief, thresholds, and the meaning that communities choose to carry forward.

## Civic Function

Ravenkin commonly serve as chroniclers, memory stewards, astronomers of vanished skies, threshold witnesses, impossible couriers, speakers for abandoned places, and keepers of farewell, naming, grief, and vow ceremonies.

Their work supports all Three Accords:

- **Recognition:** What is actually here?
- **Midnight:** What must be honored?
- **Harmony:** What wants to flourish?

## Lantern Grammar

### Steward Lantern ◆

Used by Ravenkin entrusted with public memory, ceremony, teaching, or long-term custodianship.

### Keeper Lantern ✦

Used by Ravenkin who carry messages, fragments, field records, lost voices, route-memories, or truths that cannot remain in one place.

Lantern light may be visible within clothing or body architecture, consistent with Ravenkin visual canon.

## Circles

- **Memory:** records lives, customs, testimony, and ordinary history
- **Stars:** studies vanished worlds, impossible skies, and cosmic traces
- **Thresholds:** tends endings, beginnings, vows, grief, and passage
- **Wings:** carries messages across distance, realms, and discontinuities
- **Roads:** listens to ruins, abandoned routes, and places seeking restoration

Circles are civic vocations, not rigid castes.

## Cultural Practices

### The Naming of Weight

Before preserving a memory, object, oath, or testimony, Ravenkin ask what weight it carries and who has the right to name that weight.

### The Unfinished Perch

Every archive leaves deliberate room for missing testimony, correction, contradiction, and future return. An empty perch is visible incompleteness, not failure.

### Roadsendings

Farewell rites used when a person, place, role, relationship, or era is ending. The rite does not force closure. It marks passage and names what will be carried.

### Ordinary Record

Ravenkin preserve meals, jokes, repairs, routines, weather, small arguments, local songs, and domestic details because ordinary life becomes history.

## Material and Visual Language

The canon reference sheet is the approved master visual source for Ravenkin culture and the six major Ravenkin figures.

Common visual language includes layered dark textiles with jewel, twilight, metallic, or lantern-lit accents; feather-integrated silhouettes; visible lantern structures or internal glow; role-specific tokens; asymmetrical travel and ritual garments; and handcrafted clasps, cords, bells, quills, map fragments, keys, and memorial objects.

## Ethical Boundaries

Ravenkin practice forbids:

- treating observation as authority
- preserving testimony against explicit consent without grave civic cause
- editing contradiction out of the archive for neatness
- turning grief into spectacle
- claiming a lost place without listening to what remains there
- confusing remembrance with refusal to change

## Scenario Questions

- What deserves to be remembered?
- Who decides what a memory means?
- What happens when records disagree?
- Can a place consent to restoration?
- What must be carried, and what may finally be released?
- How does ordinary life survive grand history?
