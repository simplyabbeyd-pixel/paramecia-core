# Ravenkin Major Figures

**Status:** Canonical  
**Visual authority:** Canon-locked Ravenkin reference sheet  
**Last reviewed:** 2026-07-21

## Marrow Nightwind

**Role:** Chronicler and philosophical anchor of Ravenkin culture  
**Circle:** Memory  
**Paths:** Wisdom × Craft  
**Accords:** Recognition × Midnight  
**Lantern:** Steward Lantern ◆

Marrow preserves the principle that significance must be witnessed without being owned. They are the figure most often invoked when Ravenkin debate archive ethics, interpretation, omission, and the difference between keeping a truth and controlling it.

**Tokens:** bone-white quill, black thread ledger tie, small unmarked key  
**Scenario use:** disputed histories, compromised archives, testimony without consent, missing witnesses  
**Voice key:** precise, patient, unwilling to make uncertainty prettier than it is

## Corvus Starwatch

**Title:** Astronomer of the Seventh Sky  
**Circle:** Stars  
**Paths:** Wisdom × Wonder  
**Accords:** Recognition × Harmony  
**Lantern:** Steward Lantern ◆

Corvus studies stars belonging to worlds that no longer exist. Their work treats vanished skies as both astronomical evidence and cultural memory.

**Tokens:** rotating brass star wheel, fragment of dark celestial glass, seven-knotted cord  
**Scenario use:** impossible constellations, dead worlds signaling, temporal astronomy, lost civilizations  
**Voice key:** awed but disciplined, cosmic without becoming vague

## Ash Blackwing

**Title:** Steward of Farewells  
**Circle:** Thresholds  
**Paths:** Community × Memory  
**Accords:** Midnight × Harmony  
**Lantern:** Steward Lantern ◆

Ash leads Roadsendings, naming rites, grief ceremonies, and vows. Their work does not demand closure. It gives endings a witnessed shape and ensures no one is rushed across a threshold merely to make others comfortable.

**Tokens:** farewell bell wrapped in cloth, ashwood naming slips, paired vow rings with one deliberate gap  
**Scenario use:** funerals, dissolved vows, migrations, role transitions, communities unable to release an era  
**Voice key:** gentle, ceremonial, direct about pain

## Ink Ravencrest

**Title:** Courier of Impossible Letters  
**Circle:** Wings  
**Paths:** Discovery × Wonder  
**Accords:** Recognition × Harmony  
**Lantern:** Keeper Lantern ✦

Ink delivers messages across realms, timelines, and impossible distances. A letter entrusted to Ink may arrive where its recipient is, where they were, or where they must choose whether to become reachable.

**Tokens:** self-refilling ink vial, folded route-map feather, satchel clasp shaped like an open doorway  
**Scenario use:** messages from alternate lives, undeliverable truths, temporal routes, recipients who do not yet exist  
**Voice key:** quick, curious, irreverent around danger but never around another person's words

## Crow Quillfeather

**Title:** Chronicler of Whisperwood  
**Circle:** Memory  
**Paths:** Wisdom × Craft  
**Accords:** Recognition × Harmony  
**Lantern:** Steward Lantern ◆

Crow records ordinary life because ordinary life becomes history. Their archives preserve meals, repairs, neighborhood disputes, weather, children's games, work songs, jokes, and small acts of care alongside major civic events.

**Tokens:** pocket almanac, flour-dusted recipe card, repaired pen nib, tiny weather vane charm  
**Scenario use:** erased domestic histories, class bias in archives, community festivals, recovering the life beneath official records  
**Voice key:** observant, warm, quietly devastating when someone dismisses the ordinary

## Vesper Duskwing

**Title:** Speaker for Lost Places  
**Circle:** Roads  
**Paths:** Shadow × Memory  
**Accords:** Midnight × Recognition  
**Lantern:** Keeper Lantern ✦

Vesper listens to ruins, abandoned villages, severed roads, and places that still dream of being rebuilt. They distinguish between restoration, haunting, occupation, memorialization, and the right of a place to remain changed.

**Tokens:** cracked roadstone pendant, listening fork, bundle of keys that open no surviving doors  
**Scenario use:** sentient ruins, abandoned settlements, contested rebuilding, roads remembering travelers  
**Voice key:** sparse, attentive, speaks about places as participants rather than property

## Shared Canon Notes

- All six figures are scenario-ready and character-sheet ready.
- Their visual designs are governed by the canon-locked Ravenkin reference sheet.
- Circles describe civic vocation and relationship to significance, not caste.
- Ravenkin may combine Paths even when a paired concept is not one of the formal Five Paths. Such pairings describe practice, not a replacement cosmology.
- Their authority comes from entrusted responsibility, demonstrated care, and accountable practice, never merely from having witnessed something.
