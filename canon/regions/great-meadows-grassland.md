# Great Meadows Grassland

## 1. Identity
- **Type:** Biome
- **Structural role:** Open gathering and movement landscape
- **One-sentence essence:** A broad grassland where visibility, movement, and collective presence can breathe.
- **Canon status:** Core identity locked; details provisional

## 2. Ecological Body
- **Ecological gift:** Spaciousness, orientation, gathering, and unforced movement
- **Ecological risk:** Exposure, overstimulation, and the pressure to remain visible
- **Detailed ecology:** Provisional

## 3. Relational Function
The Meadows support assembly without enclosure. They make room for many bodies, paths, and tempos while preserving sightlines and exits.

## 4. Accord Reading
- **Harmony — What wants to flourish?** Shared movement, public joy, and spacious belonging.
- **Midnight — What must be honored?** Exposure, weather, fatigue, and the right to leave the center.
- **Recognition — What is actually here?** A living commons, not an empty stage.

## 5. Stress, Repair, and Care
- **Primary stress pattern:** Too much visibility or pressure to participate
- **Repair key:** Distance, shade, quiet edges, and permission to move at one’s own pace

## 6. Atlas and Story Use
Map symbols, rituals, seasonal events, and kin anchors remain provisional for Living Atlas Part III.
