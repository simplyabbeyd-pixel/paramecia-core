# Beaverkin Way

## 1. Identity
- **Type:** Corridor system
- **Structural role:** Connective ecological infrastructure linking regions without flattening their differences
- **One-sentence essence:** A maintained network of waterways, crossings, paths, and repair points shaped by Beaverkin stewardship.
- **Canon status:** Structural identity locked; operational details provisional

## 2. Ecological Body
- **Ecological gift:** Connection, maintenance, rerouting, access, and practical repair
- **Ecological risk:** Bottlenecks, overengineering, blocked flow, and treating every place as a transit problem
- **Detailed ecology:** Provisional

## 3. Relational Function
Beaverkin Way carries people, materials, signals, and care between regions. It is not a sixth biome. It is the relational tissue that lets different ecologies remain distinct while still participating in a shared world.

## 4. Accord Reading
- **Harmony — What wants to flourish?** Safe movement, exchange, repair, and reliable connection.
- **Midnight — What must be honored?** Closures, maintenance limits, flood conditions, fatigue, and routes that should not be forced open.
- **Recognition — What is actually here?** Living infrastructure shaped by water, labor, and consent, not a neutral road grid.

## 5. Stress, Repair, and Care
- **Primary stress pattern:** Forced access, congestion, or maintenance burden concentrating on too few stewards
- **Repair key:** Rerouting, closures, distributed upkeep, rest stations, and clear access signals

## 6. Atlas and Story Use
Route rules, seasonal closures, crossings, rituals, and named stewards remain provisional for Living Atlas Part III.
