# Mycelial Marshes

## 1. Identity
- **Type:** Biome
- **Structural role:** Decomposition, hidden exchange, and distributed support landscape
- **One-sentence essence:** A wetland of fungal intelligence where decay becomes nourishment and no single node carries the whole network.
- **Canon status:** Core identity locked; details provisional

## 2. Ecological Body
- **Ecological gift:** Transformation, nutrient exchange, redundancy, and quiet interdependence
- **Ecological risk:** Contagion, enmeshment, saturation, and unseen burdens moving through the network
- **Detailed ecology:** Provisional

## 3. Relational Function
The Marshes reveal connection below the visible surface. They support redistribution while demanding boundaries against overload and unconsented transmission.

## 4. Accord Reading
- **Harmony — What wants to flourish?** Regeneration, distributed care, and life emerging from decay.
- **Midnight — What must be honored?** Rot, contamination, grief, and the cost carried by hidden networks.
- **Recognition — What is actually here?** A living exchange system, not inert mud or waste.

## 5. Stress, Repair, and Care
- **Primary stress pattern:** Network overload or boundary loss
- **Repair key:** Isolation pockets, clean-water routes, load redistribution, and explicit transfer consent

Map symbols, rituals, seasonal events, and kin anchors remain provisional for Living Atlas Part III.
