# 🌌 Core Chats Integration — Living Canon Expansion v1.0

> **Purpose:** Bind live project-chat canon formation to the versioned architecture of Paramecia / Triquel.

> **Core truth:** Nothing flourishes alone.

> **House law:** Observation does not create authority.

> **Operational truth:** Care is infrastructure.

## 1. System Roles

The project uses three primary working environments:

- **Project chats are the field site** — discovery, correction, emotional recognition, exact language, and live canon formation.
- **Notion is the inhabited studio** — readable synthesis, linked systems, review, governance, and production routing.
- **GitHub is durable memory** — versioned canon, structured data, schemas, diagrams, provenance, and rollback.

Derived presentations, images, JanitorAI packages, lorebooks, and exports may express canon, but they do not independently create authority.

## 4. Care as Runtime

Care becomes canonical only when it has form, labor, limits, and consequence.

Every care system should answer:

1. Who notices the need?
2. What signal makes the need legible?
3. What action becomes available?
4. What consent is required?
5. What exit remains visible?
6. What recovery follows?
7. What does the world remember afterward?

### Consent states

- **Green:** continue
- **Yellow:** slow, clarify, or adjust
- **Purple:** curiosity, not consent
- **Purple-Green:** curious and consenting
- **Purple-Yellow:** curious but nervous
- **Amber:** overload, recovery, and aftercare
- **Red:** stop
- **White:** rest

When stakes are meaningful, Green and Yellow should include a reason check and confirmation of the adjusted boundary.

## 5. Embercurrent Production Ecology

### Canon Surge

During a Canon Surge:

- capture exact phrases first,
- preserve corrections immediately,
- distinguish canon from exploration,
- create provisional artifacts before opening new branches,
- favor completion of one useful object over five attractive fragments.

### Shiny Vortex

New branches are captured in one sentence and parked rather than pursued during protected completion windows.

### Drop Points

A Drop Point:

- pauses expansion,
- names what has been captured,
- identifies the next safe stopping place,
- prompts one concrete care action.

### Recovery Amber

Recovery Amber lasts at least 20 minutes and contains:

- no work,
- receive mode,
- sensory settling,
- exactly one contained reward after a real completed care action.

### Re-entry Corridor

For the 20 minutes after Recovery Amber:

- return only to the last active project,
- finalize, consolidate, format, file, or gently resume,
- do not open unrelated research or major new branches,
- park emerging branches in the Shiny Vortex queue.

## 6. Living Architecture Protocol

Every important place should define six layers:

1. **Identity** — what the place is and protects.
2. **Sensing** — what states, needs, breaches, or thresholds it notices.
3. **Response** — what pressure it changes or support it offers.
4. **Permission** — what behavior it permits, slows, redirects, or refuses.
5. **Memory** — what it retains and how that memory affects future access.
6. **Consequence** — what changes in characters, relationships, institutions, or ecology.

### Cedar Finch prototype

Cedar Finch demonstrates sentient architecture without authoritarian architecture.

Its core protocol is **Name the room**.

A visitor may leave, pause, remain silent, refuse, answer a smaller question, or correct the premise. Yellow slows. Amber ends questioning and begins comfort. Red creates a full stop.

The room listens, but listening does not grant it authority.

## 11. Capture-to-Canon Loop

Every substantial project-chat output should follow this sequence:

### 1. Capture

Preserve exact language, corrections, canon locks, uncertainty, and source context.

### 2. Stabilize

Assign status, aliases, conflict notes, affected entities, and whether the material changes world truth or merely expresses it.

### 3. Route

Send the material to its correct destination:

- Canon Atlas or registry for approved truths,
- Character Constellation for people and relationships,
- Living Places for environments and institutions,
- Scenario Loom for playable pressure and consequence,
- Recovery Atlas for regulation architecture,
- Visual Archive for image canon,
- GitHub registries for structured, versioned data.

### 4. Test

Check whether the item changes memory, trust, access, place state, institutional response, ecology, or future choices.

### 5. Remember

Record source, date, status, relationships, correction history, and next production destination.

No artifact may silently promote itself into canon. Tiny crowns are confiscated at the door.

## 14. Definition of Done

An integrated canon item is complete when:

- its exact wording is preserved,
- its status is explicit,
- its relationships are linked,
- its visual source is known when applicable,
- contradictions and aliases are recorded,
- its runtime use is described,
- its care and consent implications are visible,
- its next production destination is named.
