# Recovery Atlas Operational Modules

**Status:** Canonical Working  
**Role:** Companion implementation layer for `recovery-atlas.md`  
**Core rule:** Reduce demand before adding instruction.

This document extends the Recovery Atlas with transition logic, energy-based care choices, sensory regulation, interruption recovery, evening closure, reusable Drop Points, and world-state consequences.

## 1. State Transition Logic

Use the smallest transition that restores choice.

| Current state | Primary risk | First response | Permitted next state |
|---|---|---|---|
| Baseline Current | drift or unclear priority | name one project and one action | Embercurrent or ordinary work |
| Embercurrent | skipped care, branch multiplication | capture exact language and reduce choices | Canon Surge, Drop Point, or Baseline |
| Canon Surge | canon arriving faster than it can be organized | provisional records, sources, corrections | Drop Point |
| Shiny Vortex | multiple attractive branches competing | one sentence per branch, then park | last active artifact |
| Yellow | overload approaching or interpretation mismatch | slow, clarify, reduce scope | Green, Amber, or stop |
| Amber | depleted capacity or aftercare need | no work for at least twenty minutes | Re-entry Corridor or White |
| Re-entry Corridor | premature expansion | consolidation only | Baseline Current |
| White | rest, sleep, no-demand presence | remove requests and decisions | Baseline when voluntarily resumed |
| Red | stop required | cease immediately | only user-led restart |

**Transition protection:** No system may promote Amber directly into new creation. Amber exits through White or the Re-entry Corridor.

## 2. Care Menu by Available Energy

### Near-zero energy

- sip water already within reach
- take due medication
- change posture
- use restroom
- close unnecessary tabs or lights
- receive quiet presence without questions

### Low energy

- eat a known-safe food
- change clothing or temperature
- put on sensory protection
- lie down with a timer or visible clock
- move the active artifact into a clearly named safe location

### Moderate energy

- stretch or walk briefly
- prepare food or tea
- write a Drop Point record
- perform one filing or labeling action
- set up the last active project for later return

### Returning energy

- review the Drop Point
- confirm the active project
- complete one consolidation action
- stop again before opening a new branch

Care actions are valid whether or not creative work resumes.

## 3. Environmental and Sensory Regulation Matrix

| Signal | Reduce | Add | Avoid |
|---|---|---|---|
| sound saturation | overlapping audio, alerts, conversation | headphones, ear protection, one steady sound | new media or layered playlists |
| visual saturation | extra windows, bright screens, clutter | one visible artifact, dimmer light | gallery browsing or visual research |
| body discomfort | restrictive clothing, poor posture | temperature comfort, stretch, pressure or support | treating pain as a discipline problem |
| decision fatigue | menus, branching prompts, multiple tasks | one concrete action | open-ended brainstorming |
| emotional intensity | interpretation, debate, explanation demands | accurate reflection, silence, visible exits | forcing meaning before regulation |
| time disorientation | hidden clocks, endless sessions | visible time marker and declared Drop Point | vague “keep going” momentum |

## 4. Interruption and Restart Protocol

Use after work shifts, sleep, appointments, unexpected calls, crashes, or involuntary context loss.

1. Identify the last active project.
2. Locate the last trusted artifact or commit.
3. Read the most recent Drop Point or source note.
4. Separate completed work from remembered intention.
5. Capture any new idea in one sentence only.
6. Choose one restart action: review, label, reconcile, format, or finish.
7. Do not begin with expansion.

**Restart rule:** Memory of wanting to do something is not proof that it became canon or was completed.

## 5. Evening Closure Bundle

Use when creative momentum must end for sleep or next-day functioning.

1. save the active artifact
2. record exact unresolved phrases or corrections
3. park each new branch in one sentence
4. name the next safe step
5. close research and generation tools
6. complete medication, food, hygiene, temperature, and sensory needs
7. enter White without requiring one final achievement

The evening bundle is successful when continuity is protected, not when every task is finished.

## 7. Assistant and Collaborator Error Repair

When a collaborator invents continuity, ignores a correction, merges distinct canon, or expands past capacity:

1. stop the expansion
2. state the error plainly
3. restore the exact correction or boundary
4. identify the last mutually recognized state
5. distinguish known facts from inference
6. repair the affected artifact or registry
7. resume from the repaired point only
8. preserve the correction trail

**No-gaslighting rule:** A system failure to retain context does not erase the prior correction or agreement.

## 8. World-State Consequences

Recovery and repair must alter the living world rather than remain decorative.

### Quiet Room

- reduces social and performance demands
- records no testimony without explicit permission
- may temporarily suspend role obligations
- successful use can restore access without requiring explanation

### Aftercare Atrium

- restores orientation after intensity
- records care received, not private content
- can reopen routes that were closed by depletion
- repeated unmet aftercare needs become an institutional repair obligation

### Cedar Finch

- permits refusal, silence, smaller questions, and frame correction
- records the repair point and changed behavior
- failed repair narrows access or triggers another steward
- successful repair changes future interaction permissions

### Midnight Archive

- retains cost, contradiction, grief, and last voices
- prevents cheerful summaries from erasing harm
- can block canon lock until unresolved testimony is acknowledged

### Shelf of Unfinished Things

- protects paused work from being mislabeled abandoned
- preserves provenance and the next safe step
- prevents duplicate replacement projects from claiming authority

## 9. Runtime Example

**State sensed:** Embercurrent becomes Yellow through branch multiplication.  
**Demand reduced:** New options stop; one artifact is named.  
**Consent clarified:** The creator chooses consolidation rather than expansion.  
**Care offered:** Water and sensory reduction.  
**Memory retained:** Exact corrections and parked branches are recorded.  
**Access changed:** New generation is temporarily unavailable.  
**Repair or quest:** The next session begins with reconciliation of the active artifact.

## Canon Locks

- Red means stop.
- Amber contains no work.
- Re-entry is consolidation only.
- Curiosity does not create consent.
- Observation does not create authority.
- Care is infrastructure.
- Recovery does not need to earn its place through later productivity.
- Nothing flourishes alone.
