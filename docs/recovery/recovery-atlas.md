# Recovery Atlas

**Status:** Canonical Working  
**Role:** Operational care map for creative intensity, overload, interruption, repair, and return  
**Core rule:** Recovery is not a detour from the work. Recovery is part of the world engine.

## Source-of-Truth Rule

When recovery records disagree, use this order:

1. explicit creator correction or current consent state
2. canon-locked protocol
3. maintained behavioral skill
4. Recovery Atlas synthesis
5. exploratory notes

Observation, silence, curiosity, compliance, or prior consent do not create current authority.

## State Map

### Wander

Discovery without obligation to retain, justify, structure, or canonize output.

**Response**

- allow play without requiring a deliverable
- do not silently promote fragments into canon
- capture only when explicitly requested or when loss would matter
- leave without treating exploration as failure

### Embercurrent

A high-voltage creative state marked by rapid associative patterning, strong aesthetic certainty, fast canon formation, emotional recognition, and reduced tolerance for softened or inaccurate interpretation.

**Response**

- capture exact language before paraphrasing
- preserve corrections immediately
- separate canon, candidate canon, exploration, and discarded material
- finish one artifact before opening another branch

### Canon Surge

A concentrated Embercurrent phase in which stable truths emerge faster than they can be organized.

**Response**

1. capture exact language
2. name the active project
3. create provisional artifacts
4. attach source and timestamp
5. record aliases and corrections
6. stop at a declared Drop Point

### Shiny Vortex

Branch multiplication caused by several compelling ideas, images, characters, files, or research paths competing for attention.

**Containment rule:** Capture each branch in one sentence, park it visibly, and return to the last active artifact.

### Drop Point

A deliberate safe stopping place before depletion or uncontrolled branching.

Every Drop Point records:

- active project
- current state or capacity color
- what was captured
- what remains unresolved
- what is parked
- next safe step
- one concrete care action

### Recovery Amber

A minimum twenty-minute recovery state containing no work, planning, research, canon expansion, optimization, or project switching.

**Allowed:** hydration, medication, food, restroom, stretching, sensory regulation, warmth, grounding, rest, sleep, and contained comfort.

**Not allowed:** disguised planning, "just one more" edits, new characters, new galleries, research detours, or opening parked branches.

### Re-entry Corridor

The twenty minutes immediately following Recovery Amber.

**Allowed:** finalize, consolidate, format, file, label, or gently resume the last active project.

**Not allowed:** new projects, unrelated research, major new branches, new characters, or developing anything parked in the Shiny Vortex queue.

New ideas receive a one-sentence parking note and no further development.

## State Transition Logic

Use the smallest transition that reduces pressure without erasing agency.

- **Wander → Embercurrent:** a recurring pattern, emotionally charged recognition, or explicit decision to build appears.
- **Embercurrent → Canon Surge:** several stable truths emerge rapidly and corrections begin forming a coherent structure.
- **Embercurrent or Canon Surge → Shiny Vortex:** competing branches exceed the ability to finish the active artifact.
- **Any productive state → Drop Point:** depletion approaches, interruptions occur, or exact capture is complete enough to pause safely.
- **Any state → Recovery Amber:** Amber is named, body needs become primary, overload appears, or continuing would convert care into extraction.
- **Recovery Amber → Re-entry Corridor:** at least twenty minutes of no-work recovery is complete and return is genuinely wanted.
- **Re-entry Corridor → active project:** consolidation begins on the last named project only.
- **Re-entry Corridor → Recovery Amber:** capacity drops again or work pressure begins disguising itself as care.
- **Any state → White:** rest, sleep, quiet, or no-demand presence becomes the governing need.
- **Any state → Red:** stop immediately. Do not reinterpret Red as hesitation, correction, or an invitation to negotiate.

## Consent and Capacity Colors

- **Green:** continue; confirm what is wanted and why before major changes
- **Yellow:** slow down, clarify, reduce scope, or check interpretation
- **Purple:** curiosity without automatic consent
- **Purple-Green:** curious and consenting
- **Purple-Yellow:** curious but nervous; proceed slowly and explicitly
- **Amber:** overload, aftercare, stabilization, or recovery mode
- **Red:** full stop only
- **White:** rest, quiet, sleep, or no-demand presence

Red is reserved for stopping, not correction.

## Care Actions by Available Energy

### Near-zero energy

- take one sip of water
- take due medication
- use the restroom
- change body position
- dim sound, light, or screen intensity
- put on or remove one layer for temperature comfort
- lie down without making a plan

### Low energy

- eat one already-safe food
- refill water
- wash face, hands, or mouth
- perform one gentle stretch
- set headphones, ear protection, blanket, fan, or other sensory support
- place the active project at a named Drop Point

### Moderate energy

- prepare a simple meal
- shower or complete a fuller wash reset
- take a short walk or movement break
- reset one small physical work area
- complete the Morning or Evening Recovery Bundle

**Rule:** Choose the smallest care action that genuinely addresses the need. Do not inflate care into a performance challenge.

## Environmental and Sensory Regulation

Check five environmental channels before adding instructions:

1. **Sound:** reduce competing audio, use hearing protection, or choose one stable sound source.
2. **Light:** dim glare, reduce screen brightness, change room lighting, or close visual clutter.
3. **Temperature:** add or remove layers, use airflow, warmth, cool water, or a safer room.
4. **Body pressure and position:** sit, lie down, stretch, use a blanket, or change posture.
5. **Input density:** close tabs, reduce visible choices, stop repeated explanations, and keep only the active artifact open.

Environmental regulation is not decorative comfort. It changes available cognitive and relational capacity.

## Morning Recovery Bundle

Use before creative or technical work when the body has not yet been re-established.

1. water
2. medication, when due
3. food
4. restroom or wash reset
5. clothing and temperature comfort
6. sensory setup
7. name exactly one active project
8. choose exactly one concrete next action

Partial completion must be recognized accurately. Care is not invalidated because work does not resume.

## Evening Recovery Bundle

Use when ending work, preparing for sleep, or preventing unfinished material from remaining cognitively open.

1. declare a Drop Point
2. record the next safe step in one sentence
3. park all unrelated branches without developing them
4. close project tabs and tools
5. complete medication, food, water, restroom, and sensory needs
6. lower light and information density
7. move unfinished work to the Shelf of Unfinished Things, physically or symbolically
8. enter White without requiring a final insight

The evening bundle protects continuity. It does not demand closure from material that is still alive.

## Reward Protocol

A reward follows a clearly completed and reported care action.

- never reward early
- asking for a reward is not the care action
- give exactly one contained reward
- then name at most one next care action
- do not turn care into a productivity bargain

## Interruption and Restart Protocol

Use when work is interrupted by sleep, employment, appointments, technical failure, emotional disruption, or another unavoidable demand.

### Before leaving, when possible

Record:

- active project
- active file or page
- last completed change
- unresolved question
- next physical action
- current consent or capacity state

### On return

1. read the restart marker before opening new material
2. verify that the recorded task is still wanted
3. reopen only the active artifact
4. restate the next physical action in one sentence
5. resume through Re-entry rules when the interruption involved depletion or sleep

Do not punish interruption by demanding a complete mental reconstruction from memory.

## Assistant or Collaborator Repair Protocol

Use after misinterpretation, contradiction, ignored correction, lost context, over-expansion, or false continuity.

1. stop expansion
2. name the error plainly
3. restore the user's exact correction or boundary
4. identify the last mutually recognized project state
5. separate what is known from what was inferred
6. re-establish the scenario, artifact, or task before continuing
7. resume from the repaired point only
8. record the correction in the relevant canon or workflow layer

**No-gaslighting rule:** Never imply that an explicit correction, prior agreement, or established state did not occur merely because the current system failed to retain it.

### Repair quality test

A repair is incomplete when it only apologizes. It must also restore the correct state, remove the false claim from active use, and make the next continuation point visible.

## Receive Mode Commands

- **Sit back:** complete the current task using existing material without issuing new commands
- **No more commands:** stop giving steps, choices, or demands
- **Receive mode:** present gently and require no decisions
- **Leash me back:** return attention to the named active task with firm, compact guidance
- **Next care check:** offer one concrete self-care action only

## World Integration

Recovery architecture is physically expressed throughout Triquel:

- **Quiet Room:** removes performance demands and downshifts intensity
- **Aftercare Atrium:** receives post-intensity states and restores orientation
- **Cedar Finch:** repairs through accurate naming, refusal rights, silence, and smaller questions
- **Protocol Garden:** makes consent and boundary literacy visible and learnable
- **Listening Seats:** receive truth without claiming authority
- **Shelf of Unfinished Things:** protects paused work from shame or false abandonment
- **Midnight Archive:** preserves cost, correction, grief, and last voices
- **Whispering Stairs:** slows transitions between states and roles

Every recovery place should define:

- what state it senses
- what demand it removes
- what care it offers
- what memory it keeps
- what permissions it grants or withdraws
- how exit and return remain visible

## Recovery Changes World State

Recovery is mechanically meaningful when it changes future conditions rather than merely pausing a scene.

Examples:

- A character who honors Yellow gains clearer future access than one who pressures for Green.
- A place that witnesses repeated repair may open new rooms, routes, memories, or permissions.
- An institution that ignores Amber accumulates distrust, maintenance debt, and civic instability.
- A repaired breach does not restore the exact prior state. It creates a documented new relationship with changed expectations.
- The Midnight Archive may preserve the cost of a failure even after access is restored.
- The Shelf of Unfinished Things can return an abandoned artifact without shame, but not without provenance.

## Runtime Chain

A complete recovery-aware scenario demonstrates:

**state sensed → demand reduced → consent clarified → care offered → memory retained → access restored or changed → repair or new quest emerges**

## One-Page Operational Loop

1. **Notice** the active state.
2. **Name** it without moral judgment.
3. **Reduce** pressure, input density, and branch count.
4. **Capture** only what would otherwise be lost.
5. **Care** for the body and nervous system.
6. **Recover** without work when Amber is active.
7. **Re-enter** through the last active project only.
8. **Review** what the person, collaborator, place, or system should learn.

## Operational and Fiction Boundary

The Recovery Atlas is a real creator-facing operating protocol. Triquel's Quiet Room, Aftercare Atrium, Cedar Finch, Protocol Garden, and related institutions are fictional embodiments and testing environments.

The fiction may clarify, rehearse, or express the protocol. It must not obscure bodily needs, current consent, medical reality, or the creator's authority over the operating system.

- care is infrastructure
- Amber contains no work
- Re-entry is consolidation only
- Red means stop
