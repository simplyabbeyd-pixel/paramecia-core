# Recovery Atlas v0.3

## Purpose

The Recovery Atlas is the operational care map for creative intensity, overload, interruption, repair, and return. It translates regulation protocols into repeatable architecture without turning care into punishment or productivity theater.

> **Primary rule:** Recovery is not a detour from the work. Recovery is part of the world engine.

## Canon Status

- **Status:** Canon
- **Pass:** System / Recovery Architecture
- **Scope:** Project operations and Triquel care-ecology correspondence
- **Source authority:** Explicit creator corrections and active project protocols
- **No new branches:** Yes, until field-tested

## State Map

### Embercurrent
A high-voltage creative state marked by rapid association, strong aesthetic certainty, fast canon formation, and reduced tolerance for softened or inaccurate interpretation.

**System response**
- Capture exact phrases before summarizing.
- Preserve corrections as first-class records.
- Separate canon, attractor, seed, and exploration.
- Finish one artifact before opening another branch.

### Canon Surge
A concentrated Embercurrent phase in which stable truths emerge quickly.

**System response**
- Create provisional records immediately.
- Attach source and timestamp.
- Record aliases and corrections.
- Mark visual, narrative, and protocol locks separately.

### Shiny Vortex
Branch multiplication caused by several compelling ideas competing for attention.

**System response**
- Capture each branch in one sentence.
- Park it in a visible queue.
- Return to the last active artifact.
- Do not confuse capture with commitment.

### Drop Point
A deliberate pause before depletion or uncontrolled branching.

A complete Drop Point includes:
1. What was captured
2. What remains open
3. The next safe step
4. One concrete care action

### Recovery Amber
A minimum twenty-minute recovery state containing no work, planning, research, canon expansion, or project switching.

**Allowed:** hydration, medication, food, bathroom, stretching, sensory regulation, warmth, rest, and receiving contained comfort.

**Not allowed:** disguised planning, “just one more” edit, or opening a new character, gallery, research path, or lore branch.

### Re-entry Corridor
The twenty minutes immediately following Recovery Amber.

**Allowed:** finalize, consolidate, format, file, or gently resume the last active project.

**Not allowed:** new projects, unrelated research, new major ideas, new characters, or new galleries. New ideas are captured in one sentence and parked.

## State Transition

`Embercurrent → Canon Surge or Shiny Vortex → Drop Point → Recovery Amber → Re-entry Corridor → Field Review`

At any point:
- **Red** stops the process.
- **Amber** ends demands and begins recovery.
- **White** suspends all expectations.

## Consent and Communication States

- **Green:** continue
- **Yellow:** slow down, clarify, or reduce intensity
- **Purple:** curiosity without consent
- **Purple-Green:** curious and consenting
- **Purple-Yellow:** curious but uncertain
- **Amber:** overload, aftercare, or recovery mode
- **Red:** stop
- **White:** rest and no demands

Never treat curiosity, silence, compliance, or prior consent as current permission.

## Care Actions by Energy

### Very low energy
- Drink water already within reach.
- Take due medication.
- Use the bathroom.
- Lie down or reduce sensory input.
- Receive one simple grounding statement.

### Low energy
- Eat a known-safe food.
- Adjust temperature, lighting, clothing, or sound.
- Stretch one body area.
- Move unfinished work to the Shelf of Unfinished Things.

### Moderate energy
- Take a shower or complete a sensory reset.
- Prepare a simple meal or tea.
- Walk briefly or perform gentle movement.
- Complete a Drop Point note.

## Environmental Regulation Matrix

| Signal | Remove | Offer | Avoid |
|---|---|---|---|
| Noise overload | layered audio and conversation | headphones, quieter room, single voice | repeated questions |
| Visual overload | clutter and competing screens | dimmer light, one visible task | new galleries or dashboards |
| Body depletion | work demands | water, food, medication, bathroom, rest | bargaining for one more task |
| Branch overload | open tabs and choices | one active artifact and parking queue | brainstorming expansions |
| Emotional rupture | interpretation and defense | exact recap, correction, visible repair | minimizing or reframing the error |

## Interruption and Restart Protocol

1. Preserve the last active artifact and exact stopping point.
2. Record what changed during the interruption.
3. Do not demand immediate context reconstruction from the creator.
4. Re-enter through a brief factual recap.
5. Resume only the smallest unfinished action.
6. Open no new branch until orientation is restored.

## Repair After Assistant or Collaborator Error

1. Stop the incorrect direction.
2. Recap the relevant prior context accurately.
3. Name the specific error without excuses.
4. Restore the creator’s correction as the active authority.
5. Identify any artifacts contaminated by the error.
6. Repair those artifacts before continuing expansion.
7. Re-establish the active scenario, state, or project boundary.

### No-gaslighting rule
Never imply the correction was unclear when the record shows it was explicit. Never soften a contradiction into a “difference in interpretation.”

## Reward Protocol

A reward follows a clearly completed care action and the creator reporting it.

- Asking for a reward is not completion.
- Rewards are not given early or retroactively.
- Provide exactly one contained reward after each completed action.
- Then name the next concrete care move.

## Morning Recovery Bundle

1. Stretch
2. Water
3. Medication
4. Food
5. Dress for the day
6. Name one concrete deliverable
7. Choose one pass: canon, system, scene, or artifact

## Evening Recovery Bundle

1. Record the Drop Point.
2. Park new branches.
3. Save and close the active artifact.
4. Complete body care.
5. Reduce sensory intensity.
6. Enter White or Amber with no new demands.

## Triquel Correspondence

- **Quiet Room:** removes performance demands
- **Aftercare Atrium:** restores orientation after intensity
- **Listening Seats:** witness truth without forced explanation
- **Shelf of Unfinished Things:** preserves incomplete work without shame
- **Protocol Garden:** rehearses communication and boundary literacy
- **Midnight Archive:** preserves cost, correction, grief, and last voices
- **Whispering Stairs:** slows transitions between states and roles

Every recovery place must state what it senses, what demand it removes, what care it offers, what memory it keeps, and how exit and return remain visible.
